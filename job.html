Before Job Search

What you should prepare before your job search

01

Work on your personal projects

Personal projects are a good indicators that you put in the time to self-learn and also a good way to practice the skills you have in your bag. I personally learn the most by working a project as I would brainstorm how I would implement a function or UI design. From an interviewer perspective, projects are better indicators showing your understanding of a concept or a language than reading couple lines of what you've learned in school.

02

Prepare a template for job application

You will need to dedicate your CV and resume for a position, but I'd recommend having a template ready for both of them per position type you are applying to. If you are applying for QA, SET, and Dev position, You will have different experience and skills to include in your application. Prepare a template ahead of time and that will save you a lot of time when you start applying.

03

Start your job search!

After you followed the above two steps, you are good to start looking for your co-op jobs! Langara College's C3 is a great place you can apply for jobs, but I highly recommend you take a look at other popular job listing sites such as indeed, glassdoor, and ca.talent.com. Not all co-op job listings are on C3, you should absolutely apply to as many positions as you can.

Tips for Your Job Search

What you should do during your job search

01

Write Good Cover Letters

Writing a good cover letter is very essential. Out of hundreds of co-op students, you will want to stand out and get selected for the next round of interview. Write a detailed cover letter dedicated for the company you are applying to in a formal manner. If you need help with writing cover letters, you should reach out to Langara College co-op advisors as they provide 1 on 1 with students.

02

Dedicate your resume for the position

Many students may know to write a dedicated coverletter for a position if you are serious about them. One thing most students overlook is your resume should just as be dedicated to a role as your cover letter. You will be (ideally) looking for a position that requires different skills and programming languages. Read the position's candidate expectations and highlight what they are looking for in you in your resume.

03

Study the company before interviews

Take your time to study the company you are applying to. You obviously know what the companies you are applying to is about and preferrably most of their features and how they work. Prepare some unique positive insights on their product and the company.
